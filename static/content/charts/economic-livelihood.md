# Economic & Livelihood

Page-level description and chart-specific content for the Economic & Livelihood dashboard.

---

## page

Tracking how Indonesians perceive their economic situation, expectations for the future, and coping strategies.

---

## self-poverty

Subjective poverty measures the percentage of households that self-identify as poor. This self-classification captures how people perceive their own economic status, which may differ from official poverty lines.

---

## sankey

Upward flows show optimism even among the poor. Most Indonesians expect to move up the economic ladder within five years, reflecting widespread aspirations for economic mobility.

---

## economic-outlook

The share of households expecting a better future has increased across all income groups. Even during economic uncertainty, Indonesian households maintain optimistic outlooks about their economic trajectory.

---

## life-sat

Higher life satisfaction is strongly associated with upward expectations about the future. Households that expect to climb the economic ladder report better satisfaction with their current situation, more stable finances, and fewer recent shocks. In contrast, households expecting to move down are more likely to have experienced financial stress, health shocks, or disruptions to basic services.

---

## poverty-entry

This analysis examines factors associated with households moving into or out of self-reported poverty status. Positive coefficients indicate factors associated with **exiting** poverty, while negative coefficients indicate factors associated with **entering** poverty.

---

## median-income

Those who identify as poor have substantially lower median per capita income compared to those identifying as middle class. The gap between self-reported classes has remained relatively stable over time.

---

## emergency-expense

Financial resilience is measured by whether households could pay an unexpected emergency expense of 300,000 Rupiah (approximately $20 USD). This captures the ability to absorb financial shocks.

---

## looking-work

Those who identify as poor are more likely to be actively seeking employment, reflecting both economic necessity and potentially higher rates of underemployment or informal work.

---

## coping-strategies

Households employ various strategies to cope with economic challenges. Tracking these behaviors over time reveals how financial stress manifests in daily decisions about savings, utilities, assets, and healthcare.
